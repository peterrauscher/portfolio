During my early career when finances were tight, I meticulously analyzed every purchase decision. However, I now recognize that my earning potential has increased significantly, making my time more valuable than the small savings generated by those exhaustive cost-benefit analyses.

The core principle: "Money is a big pool of water, and as long as you can keep the faucet pouring faster than it's draining you're in the clear."

## The Experiment

I propose an experiment for well-compensated technical professionals: spend a month paying for time-saving conveniences rather than pursuing marginal savings. Observe whether additional free time translates into meaningful career advancement or personal brand development.

## Attention as Currency

Attention itself has become scarce. With countless entertainment and information sources competing for engagement, individuals should recognize that their time represents genuine value—perhaps even more valuable than their hourly wage.

## Not About Hustle Culture

This isn't advocating for reckless spending or a "hustle culture" mentality. Rather, I encourage you to reconsider your relationship with time as your most limited resource, especially if you've strategically optimized your way into well-paying positions.
